Spanish Language Portfolio

As part of my studies, I am completing a series of portfolio entries related to my language competence. This first section documents cultural competence, especially within Latin America.

Studies of Latin American literature and language have helped me gain insight into subtle differences between English- and Spanish-speaking cultures. A good example of this is seen in the two-act play attached; Hispanic culture is influenced heavily by native cultures, in particular that of the Aztecs and Maya.

Perhaps the clearest indicator of this is their treatment and perception of time and destiny; within Mayan culture, time was viewed as cyclical, often repeating the same events within each cycle.

At present, I plan to continue reading Spanish literature, and as time and resources permit, travelling to Latin America and Spain, both to study cultures and to better understand local language.

Cultural Product: Study notes for a final. In this class we read several Latin American texts, and were required to provide analysis and critique, particularly with respect to historical and cultural implication. These notes reflect that.

Cultural Practices: Two-act play. While not the central focus of this work, an understanding of Latin American concepts of birth, marriage, death, and time act as a background to the work.

About

Study notes for final: Spanish 339

Throughout this class we read several works by Latin American authors, including La casa de Bernardo Alba, Las meninas, and El sombrero de tres picos. As part of our final we were requested to write a short-form essay answering one of several questions. These notes were written up in preparation for the final, and reflect an ability to not simply understand the literal meaning of the texts, but to dig deeper into the relevant cultural, political, and historical contexts and understand what the authors intended to say with their creations.

  • Class: Spanish 339
  • Date: April 2012
  • Objective: Cultural Competence: Products
